Landmarks

Salvadoran landmarks include ancient Mayan ruins like Tazumal, the perfectly preserved "Pompeii of the Americas" at Joya de Cerén, and the well-preserved colonial town of Suchitoto. Natural landmarks feature the stunning crater lake of Coatepeque and the breathtaking views from Cerro Verde National Park, part of a larger chain of volcanoes. Modern and historical city landmarks include the Metropolitan Cathedral of San Salvador and the neoclassical Palacio Nacional.

Famous Locations

El Salvador features natural wonders like the black sand beaches of El Tunco and Playa Las Flores, and the volcanoes in Los Volcanes National Park where visitors can hike the Santa Ana volcano or walk through the cloud forest of Cerro Verde. Culturally, the colonial town of Suchitoto offers charming streets and art, while the ruins of Tazumal showcase ancient Maya history and the nearby Joya de Cerén archaeological site is a preserved "Pompeii of the Americas". The scenic Ruta de las Flores route is known for its coffee plantations and vibrant flowers, linking charming villages and beautiful landscapes.
